Agentlize HRM uses **role-based access control (RBAC)** to ensure every person on your team sees exactly what they need — and nothing they shouldn't. Each user is assigned one of four built-in roles when they're invited to the workspace. Roles determine which modules a person can access, what actions they can take, and which records they can view or edit.

This page explains each role in detail, shows you how to assign or change roles, and describes the self-service capabilities available to employees and managers.

---

## Built-in roles

The table below summarizes the four roles available in Agentlize HRM and their primary areas of responsibility.

| Role | Description | Key Permissions |
|---|---|---|
| **Owner** | The workspace creator and ultimate administrator. There is exactly one Owner per workspace. | Full access to all modules, settings, and billing. Can delete the workspace and transfer ownership. |
| **HR Admin** | HR professionals responsible for day-to-day people operations. | Manage all employee records, run payroll, configure leave policies, manage recruitment pipelines, create performance review cycles, and generate all reports. Cannot access billing. |
| **Manager** | Department heads and team leads who oversee a group of employees. | View and approve leave for their assigned team, view team attendance records, run team-level reports, and participate in performance reviews as a reviewer. Cannot run payroll or access other departments' data. |
| **Employee** | Individual contributors with self-service access only. | View own payslips and attendance records, apply for leave, update personal profile information, submit performance self-assessments, and view their own goals. |

<Info>
  There can only be **one Owner** per workspace. If you need to transfer ownership to another person — for example, when a key HR leader changes roles — please contact [support@agentlize.com](mailto:support@agentlize.com). Ownership cannot be transferred from within the app settings.
</Info>

    | **Payroll** | Create, edit, approve, and finalize payroll runs. Configure tax tables, deductions, and pay schedules. Access all payslips. |
    | **Leave** | Configure leave policies and accrual rules. View, approve, reject, or override any leave request. |
    | **Recruitment** | Create and publish job postings. Manage all applicants and pipelines. Send offer letters. |
    | **Performance** | Create and manage review cycles. View all feedback and evaluation reports. |
    | **Settings** | Full access, including billing, SSO configuration, integrations, and workspace deletion. |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="HR Admin">
    HR Admins have full operational HR access but cannot manage billing or workspace-level settings.

    | Module | Permissions |
    |---|---|
    | **Employee Management** | Create, edit, and archive employee records. Upload and manage documents. Cannot permanently delete records. |
    | **Attendance** | View and edit attendance records for all employees. Configure shifts. Cannot delete historical attendance data. |
    | **Payroll** | Create and approve payroll runs. Configure deductions and allowances. View all payslips. Cannot modify tax tables (Owner only). |
    | **Leave** | Create and edit leave policies. Approve or reject any leave request. View leave balances for all employees. |
    | **Recruitment** | Full access to job postings, applicant pipelines, and offer letter generation. |
    | **Performance** | Create review cycles, assign reviewers, and view all evaluation reports. |
    | **Settings** | Can manage departments, custom fields, and notification settings. **Cannot** access billing or SSO. |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Manager">
    Managers have visibility and approval rights scoped to the department(s) they are assigned to.

    | Module | Permissions |
    |---|---|
    | **Employee Management** | View profiles of employees in their assigned department(s). Cannot create, edit, or archive records. |
    | **Attendance** | View attendance records for their team. Cannot edit records or configure shift rules. |
    | **Payroll** | No access. Managers cannot view salary information or payslips for their team. |
    | **Leave** | Approve or reject leave requests from employees in their department. View team leave calendars and balances. |
    | **Recruitment** | Can be added as a hiring collaborator on specific job postings. Can provide interview feedback. Cannot create postings. |
    | **Performance** | Can conduct performance reviews for direct reports. View their own review results. Cannot create or configure review cycles. |
    | **Settings** | No access. |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Employee">
    Employees have self-service access limited to their own data.

    | Module | Permissions |
    |---|---|
    | **Employee Management** | View and update their own personal profile (name, address, emergency contacts, bank details). Cannot edit employment terms or salary. |
    | **Attendance** | View their own clock-in/clock-out history and attendance summary. Cannot edit records. |
    | **Payroll** | View and download their own payslips. Cannot see salary information for any other employee. |
    | **Leave** | Apply for leave, view their leave balance for each leave type, and track the status of their requests. |
    | **Recruitment** | No access to the recruitment module. |
    | **Performance** | Submit self-assessments, view their own review results, and update personal goals. |
    | **Settings** | Can update their own notification preferences and password only. |
  </Tab>

## Assigning and changing roles

You can change a team member's role at any time from the Team Members settings page. Role changes take effect immediately — the user's session is updated on their next page load.

1. Navigate to **Settings → Team Members**.
2. Find the team member whose role you want to change and click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) on their row.
3. In the **Edit Member** panel, open the **Role** dropdown and select the new role.
4. Click **Save Changes**.

The team member receives an email notification informing them that their role has been updated.

<Warning>
  Downgrading a user's role (for example, from HR Admin to Employee) immediately removes their access to restricted modules. If they have any in-progress tasks — such as a draft payroll run or an open recruitment pipeline — make sure those are handed off before changing the role.
</Warning>

## What employees can self-service

Employees can handle the following tasks entirely on their own through the **My Portal** section of the app, without needing to contact HR:

- **View and download payslips** — Access the full history of monthly payslips, including gross pay, deductions, and net pay breakdowns.
- **Apply for leave** — Submit leave requests for any configured leave type, attach supporting documents if required, and track approval status in real time.
- **Update personal information** — Edit their home address, phone number, emergency contact details, and bank account information for payroll.
- **View attendance records** — Review their own daily attendance log, including clock-in and clock-out times, total hours worked, and any flagged anomalies.
- **Submit performance self-assessments** — Complete their section of a review cycle when invited by HR or their manager.
- **Track goals** — Create personal goals, update progress, and align them to department objectives when the feature is enabled.

## What managers can do

Managers are empowered to handle day-to-day team operations without HR involvement:

- **Approve or reject leave requests** — Receive notifications when a direct report applies for leave. Review the team leave calendar for conflicts before making a decision, and add a comment to explain any rejection.
- **View team attendance** — Monitor daily attendance for everyone in their department, spot patterns of lateness or absenteeism, and flag issues to HR.
- **Run team reports** — Generate attendance and leave summary reports scoped to their department for any date range.
- **Provide performance feedback** — Conduct structured reviews for direct reports during an active review cycle, including rating competencies and writing qualitative comments.
- **Collaborate on hiring** — When added to a job posting as a hiring collaborator, review candidate profiles, submit interview scorecards, and recommend candidates for progression.
