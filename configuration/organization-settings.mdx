Your organization settings are the foundation of your Agentlize HRM workspace. To access them, click the **Settings** icon in the left sidebar, then select **Organization**. From here you can configure your company profile, localize the platform for your region, define standard working hours, set your fiscal year, and manage data and privacy preferences. Changes made here affect the entire workspace unless individual users have overridden their own preferences.

---

## Company Profile

Your company profile information appears across the platform — on payslips, official documents, and the employee self-service portal. Keep this information accurate and up to date.

<ParamField path="company_name" type="string" required>
  The legal name of your organization. This name is displayed across the platform and printed on all payslips and official HR documents. Use your full registered company name here.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="company_logo" type="file">
  Upload your company logo in **PNG** or **JPG** format. The recommended size is **200 × 200 px** — larger images will be automatically resized. Your logo appears on payslips and in outgoing email notifications sent to employees. If no logo is uploaded, the platform uses a default Agentlize HRM badge.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="company_address" type="string">
  Your organization's registered business address. This address is printed on payslips and any official documents generated by the platform, such as employment certificates. Enter the full address including city, state/province, and postal code.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="website_url" type="string">
  Your company's public website URL (e.g., `https://www.yourcompany.com`). This is displayed on the employee self-service portal as a reference link. This field is optional and does not affect any platform functionality.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="industry" type="string">
  Select the industry that best describes your organization (e.g., Technology, Healthcare, Retail, Finance). Your industry selection is used to power **benchmark reporting**, which lets you compare your HR metrics — such as headcount growth, turnover rates, and leave utilization — against anonymized industry averages.
</ParamField>

## Localization

Localization settings ensure the platform presents dates, times, currencies, and language in the format that your team expects. These settings apply workspace-wide but can be overridden by individual users in their own profile preferences.

<ParamField path="default_language" type="string">
  The default UI language for all users in your workspace. When a new user logs in for the first time, they will see the platform in this language. Individual users can override this setting from **Profile → Preferences**. Currently supported languages include English, French, Spanish, German, Arabic, and Portuguese.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="timezone" type="string">
  The primary timezone for your workspace. All attendance timestamps, payroll cutoff times, and scheduled report generation use this timezone. Select the timezone that matches your main office location. If you operate across multiple timezones, you can assign per-employee timezones in the employee profile.

  <Warning>
    Changing your workspace timezone **after payroll has already been run** can cause timestamp discrepancies in historical attendance records and payroll reports. If you need to change your timezone, please **contact Agentlize HRM Support** before making this change so they can guide you through the migration safely.
  </Warning>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="date_format" type="string">
  Choose how dates are displayed throughout the platform. Options:
  - `DD/MM/YYYY` — used in most of Europe, Asia, Africa, and Latin America
  - `MM/DD/YYYY` — used in the United States and a few other regions

  This setting affects date display only and does not change how dates are entered or interpreted in imports and exports.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="currency" type="string">
  The default currency used for all payroll calculations and compensation fields (e.g., `USD`, `EUR`, `GBP`, `NGN`). Individual employee salaries can be set in a different currency — the workspace default is used when no per-employee currency is specified.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="first_day_of_week" type="string">
  Determines which day calendar views start on. Options are **Sunday** (default in North America) and **Monday** (default in most of Europe and Asia). This affects leave calendars, attendance week views, and scheduling tools.
</ParamField>

## Working Hours

Working hours settings define your organization's standard schedule. These values are used to calculate overtime, determine expected attendance, and inform payroll calculations.

<ParamField path="standard_hours_per_day" type="number" default="8">
  The standard number of working hours in a single workday. This is used as the baseline for attendance tracking and overtime calculations. The default value is **8 hours**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="working_days_per_week" type="number" default="5">
  The number of standard working days per week (e.g., 5 for Monday–Friday). This figure is used in leave balance calculations, payroll proration, and expected attendance reports.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="overtime_threshold_daily" type="number">
  The number of hours an employee must work in a single day before additional hours are counted as overtime. For example, if this is set to **8**, any hours worked beyond 8 in a day are classified as overtime.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="overtime_threshold_weekly" type="number">
  The total number of hours an employee must work in a week before weekly overtime applies. This is checked independently of the daily threshold — whichever condition is met first triggers the overtime rate.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="overtime_rate_multiplier" type="number" default="1.5">
  The pay multiplier applied to overtime hours. A value of **1.5** means employees are paid 1.5× their normal hourly rate for overtime hours. Common values include `1.5` (time-and-a-half) and `2.0` (double time). This multiplier is applied automatically during payroll processing.
</ParamField>

## Fiscal Year

<ParamField path="fiscal_year_start_month" type="string">
  The month in which your organization's fiscal year begins (e.g., **January**, **April**, or **July**). This setting affects:
  - Annual HR and payroll reports, which are grouped by fiscal year
  - Leave year resets — employee leave balances reset or carry over at the start of each fiscal year
  - Benchmark comparisons in analytics, which are aligned to your fiscal calendar

  If you change this setting mid-year, existing reports will retain their original fiscal year groupings and only new data will reflect the updated fiscal year.
</ParamField>

## Data & Privacy

Agentlize HRM gives you full control over your workspace data. You can export a complete copy of your data at any time, and you can request account deletion if you no longer need the workspace.

### Exporting Your Data

To export all HR data from your workspace:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Organization Settings">
    Go to **Settings → Organization → Data & Privacy**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request Data Export">
    Click **Export All Data**. Choose your preferred format: **CSV** (for use in spreadsheets) or **JSON** (for programmatic use or migration to another system).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the Export">
    Agentlize HRM will prepare your export. You will receive an email with a secure download link within a few minutes for smaller workspaces, or up to 30 minutes for large datasets. The download link expires after **48 hours**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The export includes all employee records, payroll history, leave records, attendance data, and documents. Passwords and payment card details are never included in data exports.
</Note>

### Account Deletion

If you wish to permanently delete your Agentlize HRM workspace and all associated data, please **contact Agentlize HRM Support** at support@agentlize.com. Deletion requests are subject to a **30-day grace period** during which the deletion can be cancelled. After the grace period expires, all data is permanently and irreversibly deleted in accordance with our data retention policy.

<Warning>
  Account deletion is **permanent**. After the 30-day grace period, there is no way to recover employee records, payroll history, or any other data. Make sure to export all data you need before confirming the deletion request.
</Warning>
